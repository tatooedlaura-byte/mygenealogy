# Possible Duplicates

People in this file who **might** be the same individual but have NOT been merged. Each entry lists the candidates, what suggests they could be the same, and what would resolve it.

The default is to keep them separate. Only merge after evidence — never on the strength of name alone.

---

## Peter Best (m. Cristina Whitenbarger, 1779) ←→ Peter Bess (1832 will, wife Leannah)
- **Candidates:** `peter-best` and `peter-bess-elder`
- **Why they might be the same:** Both in Lincoln County, NC. The 1779 Peter would be ~50–70 by 1832, plausible for a will. Surname spelling (Best/Bess) is fluid in NC records.
- **Why they might be different:** Wife names differ (Cristina Whitenbarger 1779 vs. Leannah 1832). Could be remarriage after first wife's death, OR could be father/son.
- **What resolves it:** A burial record or court statement linking the names. Or a marriage between Peter and Leannah after 1779 (would imply Cristina died).

## Polly Slinkard (A) ←→ "Polly Bess" (Jacob Bess Sr.'s wife hypothesis)
- **Candidates:** `polly-slinkard-a` (Jacob Slinkard MO's minor daughter, ~1813–1816) ←→ historical hypothesis "Polly Bess" (wife of a Jacob Bess, died ~1850)
- **Why this hypothesis exists:** Same geographic cluster (Cape Girardeau), matching timeframe, Polly naming consistency, disappearance from Slinkard records after marriage period.
- **Status:** SUPPORTED only — not proven.
- **Why it might be wrong:** This file's current model has Jacob Bess Sr.'s wife as Barbara Slinkard (PROVEN via Jacob Miller guardianship), NOT Polly. So either:
  - Polly hypothesis is wrong / replaced by the Barbara identification, OR
  - Polly is a DIFFERENT Bess wife (e.g. Jacob Jr.'s wife), OR
  - Polly = Barbara (nickname), which would collapse `polly-slinkard-a` into `barbara-slinkard`.
- **What resolves it:** Marriage record Slinkard → Bess. A probate or deed naming Jacob Bess's wife by her given name.

## Catharine Slinkard variants
- **Candidates:**
  - `catharine-slinkard-b` (Daniel's heir, 1838 probate)
  - `catharine-slinkard-smith` (m. Ruben Smith, 29 Jan 1828)
  - `katherine-slinkard-link` (m. Aaron Link, 1834)
- **Why they might be the same:** All Cape Girardeau, all in the same window. Catharine/Katherine is the same name with spelling variants.
- **Why they might be different:** Multiple marriages = multiple women (or one woman with multiple husbands across remarriages). 1828 + 1834 = could be one woman widowed and remarried; 1838 probate Catharine could be a married woman or unmarried.
- **What resolves it:** A widowhood/remarriage record. A death record for any of them.

## James Bess
- **Candidates:** `james-bess-son` (Jacob Bess Sr.'s son) and "James Bess" recorded as husband of Sarah Bess in the partition record.
- **Why this matters:** If "James Bess" the husband is the same as Jacob's son James, this would mean Sarah married her brother — which is not the natural reading. More likely:
  - "James Bess" the husband is a cousin or unrelated, OR
  - The partition record documents two different James Besses, one as son and one as Sarah's husband.
- **What resolves it:** Read the partition record carefully — does the same James appear in both roles, or is one identified as "James Bess Jr." or with a middle initial?
