# Proof Argument: Parentage of Jacob Bess Sr.

**Research question:** Who is the father of Jacob Bess Sr. (b. ~1795–1798, North Carolina; later of Missouri)?

## Known Facts (PROVEN)

1. **Jacob Bess Sr.** — 1850 U.S. Census, Cape Girardeau Co., MO: "Jacob Bess, age 52, born North Carolina." (Evidence: `ev-1850-census-jacob-bess`)

2. **Boston Best Sr.** — Estate records (Lincoln County, NC), 1809: estate administered. (`ev-1809-boston-best-estate`)

3. **Peter Best (Bess)** — 1809 estate account against Boston Best Sr.: "Estate of Boston Best Senr to Peter Best Dr … Peter Best made oath…" (`ev-1809-boston-estate-peter-account`) — direct estate involvement, SUPPORTED as immediate family.

4. **Peter Bess Estate** — 1832 will, Lincoln County, NC: estate divided "among my children" (children unnamed). (`ev-1832-peter-bess-will`) — PROVEN that Peter had children; identities not specified.

5. **Family cluster** — multiple records place Jacob Bess in the same Bess cluster as Peter and Boston descendants in NC and later in MO. (`ev-1869-boston-estate-cluster`)

## Analysis

### A. Boston → Peter
Peter Best appears directly within Boston Best Sr.'s estate records (1809), submitting sworn financial claims. This level of involvement is consistent with immediate family (typically a son or primary heir), rather than a distant associate. No conflicting individuals named Peter Best/Bess appear in the same locality/timeframe.

**Conclusion:** Peter Best is STRONGLY SUPPORTED as a son (or immediate heir) of Boston Best Sr.

### B. Peter → Jacob
Jacob Bess Sr. was born in North Carolina (~1795–1798), within the correct timeframe to be a son of Peter Best (b. ~1765–1775). Peter Bess's 1832 will leaves property to "my children" but does not name them individually. Jacob Bess:

- fits chronologically as a son
- appears within the same extended Bess cluster
- migrates to Missouri in a pattern consistent with second-generation movement

No record has been found naming an alternate father for Jacob Bess Sr. No competing Bess family structure better fits the geographic, chronological, and cluster evidence.

### C. Negative Evidence
Extensive review of probate records, estate files, court minutes, and settlement accounts has produced no document naming the children of Peter Bess individually. No evidence contradicts the proposed relationship.

## Conclusion

The identity of Peter Best/Bess as the father of Jacob Bess Sr. is SUPPORTED by strong indirect evidence:

- Direct association of Peter Best with Boston Best Sr.'s estate (1809)
- Correct generational placement
- Absence of conflicting candidates
- Consistent family cluster association
- Migration patterns aligning with second-generation movement

However, **no surviving record explicitly states the parent-child relationship.**
