# Proof Argument: Jacob Bess Sr. — Missouri Cluster and Possible Origin Group

## Subject
Jacob Bess Sr. of Cape Girardeau / Bollinger County, Missouri.

## Proven Missouri Anchor
Jacob Bess Sr. is documented in Missouri by the 1820s–1830s. An 1823 deed names "Jacob Best and Barbary his wife." The 1830 census places Jacob Bess in Cape Girardeau County, Missouri, on the same page as Peter Bess, John Bess, Frederick Bess, and nearby Crites and Kinder households.

## Proven Spouse
The 1823 Cape Girardeau County deed (`ev-1823-jacob-best-barbary-wife-deed`) explicitly names "Jacob Best and Barbary his wife." This proves Barbary/Barbara was Jacob's wife.

## Proven Estate and Children
A Bollinger County probate record from 13 Feb 1867 (`ev-1867-jacob-bess-probate-matilda-admin`) identifies "the estate of Jacob Bess, Sen., dec'd," with Matilda Bess serving as administratrix. A Bollinger County Circuit Court record from 20 Mar 1869 (`ev-1869-jacob-bess-heirs-circuit-court`) names the heir group, proving the children/heir lines of Jacob Bess Sr.: Edward B. Bess, Daniel R. Bess, James Bess, Matilda Bess, Sarah Bess, the Barbary/Blaylock line, Mary A. Bess, the Adeline/Rhodes line, and the Emanuel Bess line.

## Missouri Cluster
By 1827–1830, Jacob Bess Sr. appears in a tight Missouri settlement cluster with multiple Bess households (Peter, John, Frederick) and associated Crites, Kinder, Rhodes, and Slinkard connections. This is documented in `ev-1830-census-bess-crites-kinder-cluster-mo`.

## North Carolina Origin Question
No direct record currently proves Jacob Bess Sr.'s specific North Carolina parentage or household of origin. North Carolina Bess/Best records (the `bess-nc-cluster` and `bess-nc-origins` groups) must remain separate unless directly linked by an explicit kinship statement in a primary record.
