# Proof Argument: Slinkard Family Migration (KY → MO)

## Status

**SUPPORTED** (moderate to strong)

---

## Statement

The Slinkard family present in Cape Girardeau County, Missouri by 1823 is likely connected to a Frederick Slinker household documented in Green County, Kentucky in 1810 and 1820.

---

## Evidence

### 1. 1810 Census — Green County, Kentucky
**Record:** `ev-1810-ky-frederick-slinker-census`

Frederick ("Fredk") Slinker is head of household with multiple children across age ranges. Household composition is consistent with a man in his mid-to-late 40s with children ranging from infants to young adults.

### 2. 1820 Census — Green County, Kentucky
**Record:** `ev-1820-ky-frederick-slinker-census`

Frederick Slinker remains in Green County. Household structure shows older children (ages 16–25) with fewer young children — consistent with children leaving or having left by 1820–1823.

### 3. Missouri Records (1823 onward)
**Records:** `ev-1823-jacob-slinkard-deceased-deed`, `ev-1838-daniel-slinkard-probate`

Daniel Slinkard and the family of Jacob Slinkard (deceased) appear in Cape Girardeau County, Missouri. Jacob Slinkard was already deceased by ~1813–1816 per guardianship records, and his children were minors at that time.

### 4. Name Variation

The spelling shift Slinker (KY) → Slinkard / Slinkerd (MO) is a plausible phonetic variation. Both forms appear in the record window 1810–1823. Earlier NC records also show "Slinkard" (see `ev-1788-jacob-slinkard-heir-deed`).

### 5. Migration Timing

No Slinkard households have been identified in Missouri in 1810. By 1823 the family is established in Cape Girardeau County with land, deeds, and probate activity. The 1810–1820 Kentucky window aligns with the expected migration period.

---

## Conclusion

The geographic sequence (NC → KY → MO), name variation, and timeline are consistent with a migration of a Slinker/Slinkard family group from Kentucky to Missouri between 1820 and 1823. This is a working hypothesis, not a proven lineage.

---

## Limitations

- No direct record links Frederick Slinker (KY) to Daniel or Jacob Slinkard (MO).
- Parent-child relationships are **unproven** and must not be entered as claims.
- The name variation Slinker → Slinkard is plausible but not confirmed by any record that uses both spellings for the same individual.
- Frederick Slinker's fate after 1820 is unknown — he may have died in Kentucky, moved to Missouri, or moved elsewhere entirely.
